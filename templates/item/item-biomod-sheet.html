Attribute Modifications

Biomods can provide bonuses or penalties to attributes. Positive values enhance attributes, negative values impose penalties.

Attribute Guidelines:

  • +1 to +2: Minor enhancement (common)
  • +3 to +4: Significant enhancement (uncommon)
  • +5: Maximum enhancement (rare/experimental)
  • -1 to -2: Minor side effect or trade-off
  • -3: Significant penalty (experimental risks)

Common Biomod Effects:

  • Enhanced Vision: Improved sight in low light conditions
  • Toxin Resistance: Immunity to specific poisons or diseases
  • Aquatic Adaptation: Ability to breathe underwater or improved swimming
  • Enhanced Healing: Faster recovery from wounds and injuries
  • Environmental Tolerance: Resistance to pressure, temperature, or radiation
  • Sensory Enhancement: Improved hearing, smell, or touch sensitivity
  • Neural Interface: Direct connection to computer systems

Biomod Types:

Genetic Enhancement: DNA modifications that alter base capabilities

Cybernetic Implant: Mechanical/electronic augmentations

Therapeutic Modification: Medical treatments for health conditions

Cosmetic Enhancement: Appearance modifications and aesthetic changes

Experimental Treatment: Cutting-edge modifications with unknown risks

Installation Process:

Most biomods require professional installation by qualified geneticists, surgeons, or biomodification specialists. The process can take anywhere from hours to weeks depending on complexity.

Risks: All biomod installations carry risks including rejection, infection, neural damage, or unintended side effects.
